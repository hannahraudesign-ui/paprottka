Our History

A story that began 106 years ago.

1919
The company was founded as an iron foundry in Königsberg, East Prussia by Gustav and Ludwig Paprottka.
1923 – 1924
The iron foundry was converted into a trading and export company, shifting focus to power generation systems and woodworking machinery.
1945
Following the war, the family relocated to Itzehoe and restarted operations in West Germany — continuing with power generation systems and pump stations.
Post-war era
Manfred Paprottka joined the company and expanded its reach to Brazil and markets around the world.
Today
Alexander Paprottka continues the legacy, overseeing operations and systems in line with the company's long-established tradition of excellence.
